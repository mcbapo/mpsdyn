Detailed Description

This early implementation of ThemofieldHamiltonian was specific for the spin boson model and assumed a Hermitian coupling term spin-bath. It is now superseded by an implementation that assumes something plus its Hermitian conjugate.

A Hamiltonian containing only nearest-neighbor terms, for the particular case of the thermofield simulations. This means we have a spin site in the center of the chain, and L bosonic modes coupled to it on each side. We assume couplings of the form

virtual

Construct the MPO corresponding to the evolution operator with the splitting even-odd in the Hamiltonian. Take as argument the complex factor in the exponential, delta. For real time evolution, delta is -i*tau, for imaginary time, it should be -tau. The even term includes boson pairs 01, 23,... AND the free Hamiltonian of the central spin. The odd term includes the bosonic pairs 12,34... AND both coupling terms between spin and zero-modes, which we assume commute.
