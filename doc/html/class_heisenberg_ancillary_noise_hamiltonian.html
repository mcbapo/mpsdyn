Detailed Description

Implementation of a chain with two types of spins, , corresponding to two Heisenberg chains coupled by a term. Different to HeisenbergHamiltonian, here all the coefficients are translationally invariant. The second type of spins, when traced out, realizes a random magnetic field for the first chain.

The are due to the fact that the implementation is defined in terms of spin matrices, not sigmas, as in the parent HeisenbergHamiltonian. The implementation is done in terms of a single chain, in which and sites alternate.

Construct the MPOs corresponding to the evolution operator. In particular, we use the even-odd splitting for the sigma and tau Heisenberg terms independently, and require an additional exponential for the mixed term. Each of the methods prepares a particular term, with the given complex factor in the exponential, delta. For real time evolution, delta is -i*tau, for imaginary time, it should be -tau. The even/odd terms require a third (boolean) argument, sigma, to determine whether the term for (if true, default) or for (if false) is computed.
